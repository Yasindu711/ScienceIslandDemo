Global Collaboration for a Future in STEM

An inspiring journey of international collaboration, bringing STEM education to life through an engaging online game.

The Journey of Science Island

Science Island, hosted by Amazon Web Services (AWS), is a STEM educational game developed by Swinburne Senior Lecturer James Marshall. The game was fast-tracked for release during the COVID-19 pandemic to provide educational support for children in lockdown.

“We had planned to launch Science Island at the end of 2020, but given the COVID-19 outbreak and the number of children in social isolation, we have partnered with Amazon to launch a beta version immediately,” says Mr. Marshall.

Global Collaboration

The concept of Science Island was born in 2012 when Mr. Marshall partnered with the Kasese Humanist Primary School in Uganda. Swinburne students and international scientists worked together to create interactive content based on STEM questions from Ugandan students.

“I had this idea that Swinburne students could take a design-led approach to teaching STEM, creating cool content and fun activities to inspire children all over the world,” says Mr. Marshall.

Expanding the Partnership

The partnership with the Kasese Humanist Primary School in Uganda involved helping them develop their infrastructure and teaching resources. Over the last six years, Swinburne and partners have developed an entire campus for the school, including a computer room, a library, a vocational center, even chicken pens, with the aim of feeding the kids one egg per day. There are now 650 students at the Kasese Humanist Primary School, of which about 100 are orphans.

International Support

World-leading scientists from institutions like NASA, MIT, and the University of Cambridge contributed to the project. Their collaboration resulted in more than 20 hours of animated video, games, books, quizzes, songs, and experiments.

“We’ve even managed to get leading astrophysicist Pamela Gay to provide voice acting for the character of ‘Jean Pool’,” says Mr. Marshall.
